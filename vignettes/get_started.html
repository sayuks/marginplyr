Get started

Published

July 18, 2026

marginplyr brings SQL-style grouping sets to dplyr workflows. A single grouping specification can be executed locally, translated to native SQL, or expanded to a portable UNION ALL query.

1 Installation

Install the development version from GitHub:

# install.packages("pak")
pak::pak("sayuks/marginplyr")

# Optional packages used by this guide and its Quarto tabset example
install.packages(c("knitr", "quartabs", "tibble"))
library(marginplyr)

  ~year, ~region, ~store, ~product, ~channel, ~amount,
  2024L, "East", "E1", "A", "Online", 10,

2 The interface

.by contains fixed groups that occur in every result level. .grouping describes the dimensions that can be aggregated.

sales |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    .by = year,
    .grouping = rollup(region, store)
  )
# A tibble: 11 × 4
    year region store total
   <int> <chr>  <chr> <dbl>

 2  2024 East   E2       20
 3  2024 East   Total    30
 4  2024 Total  Total    45
 5  2024 West   Total    15

 7  2025 East   E1       12
 8  2025 East   Total    12
 9  2025 Total  Total    30
10  2025 West   Total    18
11  2025 West   W1       18

"Total" is a display label. Internally, grouping rows are identified from the grouping plan or SQL GROUPING() result, never by comparing this string.

3 Arbitrary grouping sets

grouping_sets() forms the union of explicitly requested sets. grouping_set() with no columns is the empty grouping set and produces a grand total (or one total per fixed .by group).

sales |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    .grouping = grouping_sets(
      grouping_set(region, store),
      grouping_set(region, product),

8 Composite dimensions

A nested grouping_set() inside rollup() or cube() is one indivisible dimension.

locations <- tibble(
  country = c("JP", "JP", "US"),
  state = c("Tokyo", "Osaka", "CA"),
  year = c(2024L, 2025L, 2024L),
  amount = c(10, 20, 30)
)

locations |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    .grouping = cube(grouping_set(country, state), year)
  )
# A tibble: 9 × 4
  country state year  total
  <chr>   <chr> <chr> <dbl>
1 JP      Osaka 2025     20
2 JP      Osaka Total    20
3 JP      Tokyo 2024     10
4 JP      Tokyo Total    10
5 Total   Total 2024     40
6 Total   Total 2025     20
7 Total   Total Total    60
8 US      CA    2024     30
9 US      CA    Total    30

country and state are always included or aggregated together.

9 Inspecting aggregation levels

grouping(x) returns 1 when x was aggregated away and 0 when it belongs to the active grouping set. grouping_id() combines those flags as a bit mask; the last argument is the least-significant bit.

sales |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    region_grouped = grouping(region),
    level = grouping_id(region, store, product),
    .grouping = grouping_spec(
      rollup(region, store),
      cube(product)
    )
  )
# A tibble: 17 × 6
   region store product total region_grouped level
   <chr>  <chr> <chr>   <dbl>          <int> <int>
 1 East   E1    A          22              0     0
 2 East   E1    Total      22              0     1
 3 East   E2    B          20              0     0
 4 East   E2    Total      20              0     1
 5 East   Total A          22              0     2
 6 East   Total B          20              0     2
 7 East   Total Total      42              0     3
 8 Total  Total A          37              1     6
 9 Total  Total B          20              1     6
10 Total  Total Total      75              1     7
11 Total  Total <NA>       18              1     6
12 West   Total A          15              0     2
13 West   Total Total      33              0     3
14 West   Total <NA>       18              0     2
15 West   W1    A          15              0     0
16 West   W1    Total      33              0     1
17 West   W1    <NA>       18              0     0

This also distinguishes source missing values from grouping rows. A source NA has a grouping bit of 0; an NA produced by aggregation has a bit of 1. Columns fixed with .by can also be passed to these helpers; because they are present in every grouping set, their grouping flag and bit are always 0.

10 Labels and column types

Change the report label with .margin_label:

sales |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    .grouping = rollup(region, store),
    .margin_label = "All"
  )
# A tibble: 6 × 3
  region store total
  <chr>  <chr> <dbl>
1 All    All      75
2 East   All      42
3 East   E1       22
4 East   E2       20
5 West   All      33
6 West   W1       33

When a character label is used, grouping dimensions are returned as character columns; local factors are reconstructed with the label as their first level. Use .margin_label = NULL to keep original column types and typed missing values. In that mode, use grouping() or grouping_id() to tell source and aggregation missing values apart.

For local data, .check_margin_label = TRUE prevents an existing value such as "Total" from becoming visually ambiguous. Disable the check only when the grouping bits are also retained.

11 Computed grouping columns

Grouping constructors accept column selections rather than arbitrary SQL expressions. Create an expression result first with mutate().

sales |>
  mutate(region_product = paste(region, product, sep = ":")) |>
  summarize_with_margins(
    total = sum(amount),
    .grouping = rollup(region_product)
  )
# A tibble: 5 × 2
  region_product total
  <chr>          <dbl>
1 East:A            22
2 East:B            20
3 Total             75
4 West:A            15
5 West:NA           18

This form also translates predictably for lazy database tables.

union_all_with_margins() emits one input-row copy per grouping set. nest_with_margins() returns an ordinary nested result, while nest_by_with_margins() returns its row-wise counterpart. The summarize and union verbs support local and lazy tables; the two nesting verbs support local data frames and dtplyr steps because their results contain list columns.

13 Quarto tabset reports with quartabs

quartabs turns columns of a data frame into nested Quarto tabsets. It pairs naturally with nest_by_with_margins(): detail groups, subtotals, and the grand total become tabs backed by the corresponding rows of the original data.

The chunk option results: asis is required because render_tabset() emits Quarto tabset markup. The rendered tabs below are generated when this page is built; they are not a static screenshot.

tabbed_report <- sales |>
  nest_by_with_margins(
    .grouping = rollup(region, store)
  ) |>
  mutate(
    report = list(
      data |>
        summarize(
          rows = n(),
          amount = sum(amount)
        ) |>
        knitr::kable(col.names = c("Rows", "Amount"))
    )
  ) |>
  ungroup()

tabbed_report |>
  quartabs::render_tabset(
    tabset_vars = c(region, store),
    output_vars = report
  )

14 Database execution

DuckDB and PostgreSQL currently use native GROUP BY GROUPING SETS. Native queries use hidden GROUPING() flags and apply the display label in an outer query, preserving the input type seen by summary expressions. Other or unconfirmed SQL dialects use the UNION ALL adapter. This conservative capability policy favors equal results over assuming dialect support.
